Strategic Brief // EphemeralOS

The future of AI-facing ticket systems.

The ticket as a primitive is a vestige of the human-AI handoff. As AI authors more of the work, the ticket dissolves into a projection of a deeper structure — goals decomposed and executed on shared state under drift-bounded autonomy.

// 03 · ARCHITECTURAL FIT

03Five structural alignments

PROP 01

Goal-iteration-attempt is the right abstraction

Tickets are projections of goals. EphemeralOS never made the ticket primary — it natively models the hierarchy that other systems must retrofit.

PROP 02

Blackboard via OCC scales beyond isolation

Workspace-per-ticket cannot coordinate across work. Tool-call-granular overlays + OCC let any number of agents observe each other's committed state.

PROP 03

Drift guardrails are not optional

When humans aren't PR-reviewing, drift accumulates fast. Planner-declared criteria + advisor pre-gate + binary evaluator is the structural defense.

PROP 04

Replayable packets become the audit story

Enterprises will require: why did the system act, what did each agent see, how do we reproduce the decision. Packets, by construction, answer this.

PROP 05

Source of goal is irrelevant to architecture

Today a human pastes an epic; tomorrow an upstream AI emits a goal. The interface is the same: <goal> text. No rework when human authorship ends.

OpenAI shipped autonomous coding orchestration as a category. Buyers will hit Symphony's workspace-per-ticket ceiling within a year — and look up the stack.

Engineers informally draft tickets with Claude / GPT. The formalization — AI generating the entire backlog — is months away, not years.

Audit, replay, drift bounds, compliance. As autonomy increases, these become hard requirements. EphemeralOS's architecture is them; competitors retrofit.
